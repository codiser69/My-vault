## ---<mark style="background: #FFB86CA6;">Binomial Nomenclature</mark>---
- <u>Carolus Linnaeus</u> is called the father of modern taxonomy
- it is Introduced by him, binomial nomenclature involves naming organisms with a "genus or genera or Taxon" name followed by a "species" name, both in Latins i.e. *'<u>Homo</u> + <u>sapiens</u>'*. & *'<u>Pavo</u> + <u>cristatus</u>'*
- the conventions of naming include 'capitalizing' the genus name, 'using lowercase' for the species name, and 'italicizing or underlining' the names. Name can't be less than 3 or more than 20 letters.
  ![[Screenshot_20241108-122744.jpeg]]
- ### ==*Specific Naming Conventions:-==*
- The trinomial system includes a third word for subspecies differentiation, though it is less commonly used.
- When the genus and species names are the same, the term "toponyms" applies in naming animals, while such names (termed "patronyms") are not accepted for plants.

## ---<mark style="background: #FFB86CA6;">Characteristics of the Kingdoms</mark>---

- **[[details OF specific SECTION|MONERA]]**: Includes prokaryotic organisms like bacteria; can be autotrophic or heterotrophics.
- **Protista**: Comprises unicellular eukaryotic organisms like algae and protozoans, exhibiting varied modes of nutrition's.
- **Fungi**: Non-photosynthetic organisms like mushrooms, muca and molds that decompose organic matters. 
  They have Cell walls made-up of tough complex sugar called *Chitin*. 
- **Plantae**: All multicellular and Eukaryotic with cell-wall(made of cellulode) , autotrophic plants that use chlorophyll for photosynthesis.
- **Animalia**: The largest kingdom, consisting of Eukaryotic without cell wall, multicellular, heterotrophic organisms.
