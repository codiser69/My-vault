# **<u>Constitute assembly</u>** 
- The Cabinet Mission put forward the idea to form a Constituent Assembly to draft the Constitution  of India. 
- The Assembly was designed to have members who were **partly elected** (by Provincial Legislative Assemblies) and **partly nominated** (by the princely states).

- The NEW Constituent Assembly was fixed at 299 members, as below :-
  - Indian provinces (229), and
  - Princely States (70)
- The Muslim league opposed the constitution Assembly & demanded separate Pakistan.

- Constituent assembly which had been elected the framing of the constitution held its 
	1. **<u>First meeting</u>** :- 9 dec 1946, Chaired by Dr. Sachidanand Sinha presided the inaugural meeting and declared as Interim president during 1946.(211 members participated)
	2. **<u>Second meeting</u>**:- 11 dec 1946, under the permanent chairman of constituent assembly, Dr. Rajendra Prasad, with H.C. Mukherjee as vice president. 
	Constitutional adviser of the assembly B.N.Rao, He was an jurist & appointed in 1946, responsible for preparing initial draft of the constitution.
	3. **<u>Third meeting</u>** :- 13 dec 1946, Jawaharlal Nehru presented the Objective resolution (now called Preamble) before the Constitution Assembly.
	22nd Jan 1947, Objective Resolution adopted by Constituent assembly.
 - The Assembly became a fully sovereign body, empowered by the Act of 1947 to amend or abolish any law passed by the British Parliament regarding India. 
Its primary responsibilities were to draft the constitution for a free India and enact laws for governance.

<mark style="background: #FF5582A6;">Trivia</mark> :- It is noteworthy that, Although members of the Constituent Assembly were not directly elected by the Indian people, it included representatives from all sections of society: Hindus, Muslims, Sikhs, Parsis, Anglo-Indians, Indian Christians, Scheduled Castes, Scheduled Tribes, Backward Classes, and women.

## <u>Committees of the Constituent Assembly :-</u>
To manage the workload efficiently, the Constituent Assembly established various committees focusing on specific areas of constitution-making. There were eight major committees:
1. **The Union Powers Committee** presided over *Pandit Jawaharlal Nehru.*
2. **The Union Constitution Committee** presided over *Pandit Jawaharlal Nehru.*
3. **States Committee** (Committee for Negotiating with States) presided over *Pandit Jawaharlal Nehru*
4. **The Provincial Constitution Committee** presided over *Sardar Patel.*
5. **Drafting Committee** presided by *Dr. B.R. Ambedkar.* 
6. **Advisory Committee on Fundamental Rights, Minorities and Tribal and Excluded Areas** presided by *Sardar Patel*.
    This committee had the following<u> five sub-committee</u>s:
	1. **Fundamental Rights** Sub-Committee with J.B. Kripalani as the Chairman.
	2. **Minorities Sub-Committee** with H.C. Mukherjee as the Chairman.
	3. **North-East Frontier Tribal Areas and Assam Excluded & Partially Excluded Areas Sub-Committee** with Gopinath Bardoloi as the Chairman.
	4. **Excluded and Partially Excluded Areas** (Other than those in Assam) **Sub-Committee** with A.V. Thakkar as the Chairman.
	5. **North-West Frontier Tribal Areas Sub-Committee**. r Rules of Procedure Committee presided over by Dr. Rajendra Prasad.
7. **Rules of Procedure Committee** - *Dr. Rajendra Prasad*
8. **The Steering Committee** was presided by *Dr. Rajendra Prasad.*

### <u>The Drafting Committee of the Constituent Assembly</u>
- The Drafting Committee was led by Dr. B.R. Ambedkar (set up on 29th aug 1947)
- Tasked with drafting India's Constitution using proposals from various committees.
- Dr. Ambedkar was the first person to introduce a new draft of the Indian Constitution
> - this committee Comprised of 7 Assembly members:-

> 	3. Syed Mohammad Saadullah;

> 	5. N Gopalaswamy Ayyangar; 
> 	6. Alladi Krishnaswamy Ayyar; 
> 	7. T T Krishnamachari (He replaced D P Khaitan who died in 1948).
- The process of drafting the Constituent spanned from 1946 to 1950.
- it take 141 days to prepare it's Draft.![](https://i.imgur.com/GgHGVKu.png)
- The second draft was released in October 1948.
- Final version of Constituent was adopted by Constituent assembly on 26th Nov 1949, Drafting of the constitution also completed which was celebrated as **Samvidhan Divas** 
- It officially came into effect from 26th January 1950, which has been celebrated as the **Republic Day of India.** This date was chosen to honour **Puran Swaraj Declaration (26 Jan 1950)**.
