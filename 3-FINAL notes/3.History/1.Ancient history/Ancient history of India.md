#### Veda (or Vedas)  

^7053e8

- The oldest scriptures are the Vedas. The Vedas highlight the religious, social, cultural, economic and political life during the [[Aryans & The Vedic Ages (1500 - 600 BC)#Identity Of Aryans |Aryans period]].
- **Maharshi Krishna Dvaipayana** Veda Vyasa is said to be the compiler of Vedas. 
- The number of Vedas is four. **Rig Veda**, **Sama Veda** and **Yajur Veda** are called **<u>Vedatrayi.</u>**
- <mark style="background: #FFF3A3A6;">Historical importance </mark>:- The transition from a pastoral, pre-class/caste society in the Rigveda to an Agrarian, class and caste society in the later Vedic Period is significant. texts from this era provide insights into the development of social hierarchies, the emergence of the caste system, and the formation of political territories, reflecting broader socio-economic changes. 
- The **'Sam No Varunah'** an invocation to lord Varun written in the Vedas, was adopted by the Indian Air force.
- Vedas are also known as '**Shruti**'. The reason behind is that Vedas have been passed on from one generation to another through verbal transmission.
- **The Gayatri Mantra** is given in the third mandala of the Rig Veda, which is dedicated to the *Godess Savitri.* 
- India was named '**Bharat**' often the Bharat Dynasty. The first mention of this dynasty is in **Rigveda**. 
- **Yajur Veda** is written both in prose & poetry. 
- The Yajur Veda is broadly grouped into two parts Shukla Yajur Veda and Krishna Yajur Veda. 
- The Maximum hymns of Sama Veda are taken from the Rig Veda.

# Archaeological Sources of History

^79f24b

- archaeology is a scientific discipline that involves systematically excavating ancient mounds to understand the material life of past societies based on the artifacts and remains discovered.
- it is very important to study prehistory i.e. the period before the invention of writing.
- The ==father of Indian archaeology== and first Director General of Archaeological Survey in 1871 is - *Alexander Cunningham*
- Archaeological sources are the most authentic for the study of ancient India.
- It mainly consists of inscriptions, coins, monuments, buildings, sculptures and paintings.

A. Inscriptions and Edicts
- The study of inscriptions is known as epigraphy, while the study of ancient writing styles found in inscriptions and old records is called palaeography.
- The earliest inscriptions were found at Boghazkoi in Central Asia Minor related to 1400 BC.
- Vedic deities Indra, Mitra, Varuna and Nastya (Ashwin) are mentioned in the Boghazkoi inscriptions.
- The oldest inscriptions in India belong to Ashoka, related to 3rd Century BC. Â¾ Ashokaâ€™s name is mentioned in inscriptions related to Maski, Gurjara, Nittur and Udegolam.
- The scripts of Ashokaâ€™s inscriptions are mainly Brahmi, Kharoshthi, Greek and Aramaic scripts.
- An article, written by Indo-Greek ambassador, Heliodorus on Garuda Pillar found at Besnagar (Vidisha, Madhya Pradesh) provides evidence of the development of the Bhagvat religion.
- A copper plate found at Sohgaura is said to belong to pre-Mauryan period and mentions famine relief efforts.
- Mandsaur inscriptions refer to the categories of silk weavers.
- Persepolis and Behistun inscriptions reveal that the Iranian emperor Darius had captured the valley of the Indus River.
- James Prinsep fi rstly decoded the inscriptions of Ashoka, written in Brahmi script in 1837 AD.
- The study of inscriptions is called â€˜Epigraphyâ€™.
- The first epigraphic evidence of Sati practice has been found from Eran Pillar Inscription of Bhanugupta, Madhya Pradesh.
