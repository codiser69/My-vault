## ---- Organic Evolution ----
More and more creation of organism by gradual changes from low category animal to higher animal is called **organic evolution**.  
--> There are several Evidences from morphology and anatomy regarding organic evolution. On the basis of morphology (outer appearance) and anatomy (inner structure), several evidences have been described as follows:-   
##### **1. Homologous Organs** :-
- The organs which are similar in basic structure and origin but dissimilar in function are called homologous organs, e.g., wings of bat, cat's paw, front foot of horse, human's hand and wings of birds.
- These show divergent evolution.
##### **2. Analogous Organs (Homo-plastic)**
-  The organs which are similar in shape and function but dissimilar in their origin and development. e.g., wings of insects, birds and bats, eyes of octopus and mammals. 
-  They show, convergent evolution. They evolved independently, often due to similar environmental pressures or niches.
##### **3. [[Vestigial]] Organs** 
-  These are remnants of structures that had a function in early ancestors but are now reduced or useless. For instance, the human appendix is a vestigial structure that no longer serves a significant function, unlike in some herbivores.
 - Human body has been described to possess about 100 vestigial organs. Some of these are muscles of ear pinna, canine teeth and third molar teeth, body hairs, vermiform appendix, nictitating membrane of eye, caudal vertebral (coccyx or tail bone) etc.
##### **4. Atavism or Reversion** 
 - It is the sudden reappearance of some ancestral features/traits. Appearance of thick body hair, large canines, monstral face, short temporary tails, extra nipples, Extra Toes in Horses, Teeth in Chickens etc are examples of Atavism.
 - By understanding [[atavisms]], scientists gain a deeper knowledge of how genetic inheritance and evolution work, reinforcing the connections between modern organisms and their ancient relatives. 
##### **5. Comparative Embryology:** 
 - Studying the embryonic development of different species shows that embryos of many organisms exhibit similar stages in early development, suggesting a common ancestry. 
 - For example, vertebrate embryos (including humans) show gill slits and tail structures at certain stages of development.
##### **6. Fossil Record :** 
 - Fossils provide a chronological record of past life forms and their structures, allowing scientists to trace the evolutionary history of species. 
 - The transition from fish to amphibians is well-documented by fossils showing gradual changes in morphology.
 - **lead uranium method, Radiometric dating, carbon dating, fission track and electron spin resonance** is used to determine the age of fossils.

# THEORIES OF EVOLUTION
#### a.) Lamarckism (1809) 
- <u>Jean-Baptiste de Lamarck</u> gave the idea that an organism can pass on characteristic that it acquired during its lifetime to its offspring (also known as heritability of acquired characteristics). 
- Lamarck's theory of evolution was published in *Philosophie* *Zoologique* and had four propositions, viz as  following:-
	1. Living organisms and its parts tend to increase in size due to internal force of life. 
	2. Formation of new organs is the result of a new need and new, involvement. 
	3. Individuals lose characteristics that they do not require (or use) and develop characteristics that are useful. 
	4. Individuals inherit the traits of their ancestors. 
#### b.) Darwinism (1859) 
- Darwin's theory of evolution was *Origin of Species* by Natural Selection. 
- The theory consists of five propositions, they are:- 
	1. Enormous fertility or Abundant capacity of reproduction in animals
	2. Struggle for existence 
	3. Variation and heredity 
	4. Survival of the fittest or natural selection 
	5. Origin of species.
#### c.) Neo-Darwinism: 
- After Darwinism, neo-Darwinism emerged through the incorporation of Mendel's Genetic Principles and Darwin's theories by evolutionary scientists.   
- According to this theory, various factors affect species as mutation, nature, diversity and reproduction, and due to these effects, new species of organisms are formed.

#### d.) Mutation Theory 
 - <u>Hugo de Vries</u> proposed the theory of mutation, while working on *Oenothera lamarckiana* plant. 
 - Mutations are discontinuous variations. 
 - Mutations are due to changes in chromosomes, genes and DNA. They may or may not be inherited, Therefore a new similar species may originate from a parent or ancestral species at the same time.
#### e.) Synthetic Theory 
- Neo-Darwinism emerged as the "modern synthetic theory of evolution".
- This Theory originated from the book 'Genetics and the Origin of Species' by Dobzhansky (1937) and was named by Julian Huxley (1942)
- According to it, the five basic factors are :-
	1. Gene mutation
	2. Changes in chromosome structure and number 
	3. Genetic recombination
	4. Natural selection
	5. Reproductive isolation

`First three factors are responsible for GENETICS variability.`
