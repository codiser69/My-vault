# Types of Cells
These are of two types based on Cellular structure
1. **<u>Prokaryotic Cells  (prokaryotes)</u>** :- 
	- (Pro =primitive, karyos =nucleus) 
	- These are primitive cells, have no Nucleus. i.e, [[Pasted image 20241228151134.png|Bacteria]], Blue green algae
	- Instead, some prokaryotes such as bacteria have a region within the cell where the genetic material ( DNA or RNA) is freely suspended. This region is called the **nucleoid** 
	- Membrane-bound cell organelles is Absent : Nuclear Membrane, Plastid, Lysosome, Golgibody, histone protein, Mitochondria.
2. <u>**Eukaryotic Cells  (eukaryotes)**</u> :-
	- (Eu = true, karyos = nucleus) 
	- These have a well defined nucleus and membrane bound cell organelles. These are <mark style="background: #FFB86CA6;">present in unicellular and multicellular plant and animal cells.</mark>
	- The plasma membrane is responsible for monitoring the transport of nutrients and electrolytes in and out of the cells. It is also responsible for cell to cell communication.
	- Eukaryotic cells are generally larger and more complex

### C.) Nucleus
- A Nucleus is defined as a double-membraned organelle found in eukaryotic cell that contains the genetic material in form of DNA, which is organized into chromosomes.
- the nucleus sends signals to cells to grow, mature, divide, and die, also controlling activities like protein synthesis.
- ![](https://i.imgur.com/FAZF3xq.png)
- The cell nucleus consists of a nuclear membrane (nuclear envelope), nucleoplasm, nucleolus, and chromosomes.
- ![](https://i.imgur.com/EeGkyrh.png)
- **Nucleus Envelope** : 
	- the nuclear envelope consists of phospholipids that form a lipid bilayer. 
	- The envelope maintains the nucleus's shape and regulates molecule the flow of the molecules into and out of the nucleus through nuclear pores.
- **Nucleoplasm (karyoplasm)** is the Fluid/matrix present inside the nucleus.
- **Nucleolus** - It is a distinct, spherical structure found within the nucleus, Primarily responsible for the synthesis and assembly of ribosomal RNA (rRNA) and ribosomal subunits. (which are essential for protein synthesis).  
  These ribosomes are cell organelles made up of RNA and proteins; they are transported to the cytoplasm, which is then attached to the endoplasmic reticulum.  
  The ribosome is the protein-producing organelles of a cell. The nucleolus disappears when a cell undergoes division and reforms after the completion of the cell division.
- Chromatin material is also called Chromatin reticulum.
### D.) Chromosome
- Chromosomes are the condensed form of DNA.
-  The condensation is caused by special protein called **histones**. When DNA is not condensed, it is called **chromatins**. 
- ![](https://i.imgur.com/EeNZLZu.png)
	- **Centromere** is the region of a chromosome where two sister chromatids attach. During cell division, chromosomes connect at the centromere before separating and moving to opposite poles of the cell.
	On the basis of position, centromere can be metacentric (present at centre), submetacentric (little away from centre), acrocentric (situated close to its end) and telocentric (terminal centromere).
	- **[[Pasted image 20241227163028.png|Telomeres]]** are the sequences of repetitive nucleotides found at the end of each chromosome. Their length shortens with each cell division, determining a cell's lifespan.
	A cell may undergo approximately 40 to 60 divisions by mitosis, as per Hayflick's limit. afterwards, it goes for the process of senescence and die.
	Cell Life is Influenced by stress, nutrition, health, and other conditions.
- Chromosomes are units of inheritance. In some viruses e.g. retrovirus, **RNA** is the genetic material.
- <u>**Types of Chromosome : -**</u>
	- **Lampbrush Chromosome** It was first discovered by Balbiani in 1882 and resemble the shape of a lamp brush.
	- **Polytene Chromosome** It was discovered by Balbiani in 1881 in salivary gland of Chironomus larvae.

# Nucleic Acids
 - These contain the genetic instructions used in the development and functioning of all known having organisms. 
 - These are of two types: DNA and RNA. 
 - ### Deoxyribonucleic Acid (DNA) 
	- ![](https://i.imgur.com/pNH9VMX.png)
	 - *James D Watson* and *Francis Crick* who got Nobel Prize for this discovery. 
	 - It is a long polymer made from repeating units called nucleotides. 
	 - Each nucleotide consists of a nucleoside (i.e. nitrogen base and deoxyribose sugar) and a phosphate group, joined together by phosphodiester bonds.
	 - It has [[Pasted image 20241228102327.png|four bases/strands]], i.e. adenine, guanine, cytosine and thymine. 
	 - Adenine and guanine are the purine bases; cytosine and thymine are pyrimidine bases.

- ### Ribonucleic Acid (RNA)
	- RNA helps in the synthesis process of proteins in our body by translating the genetic information encoded of DNA into protein, It is also made up of a long chain of nucleotides. Each nucleotide consists of a nitrogen, a ribose sugar, and a phosphate group. 
	- It [[Pasted image 20241228102327.png|contains uracil in place of thymine]].
	- RNA is of [[Pasted image 20241229100708.png|three types]]--
		- mRNA (messenger RNA)
		-  rRNA (ribosomal RNA)
		-  tRNA (transfer/trasciption RNA) 
	- These three RNA's take part in protein synthesis.
