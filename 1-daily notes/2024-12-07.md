# NATIONAL SYMBOLS
The National Flag (Tricolour) is rectangular in shape and consists of three colours. 
The ratio of the length of the flag to its width is 3:2. 
The colour of the top band is deep saffron (symbol of strength and courage), the middle band is white (symbol of peace and truth) and the bottom is Dark green (symbol of fertility, growth and auspiciousness of the land).
In the centre of the white band is a navy blue wheel which represents the chakra. It has 24 spokes, and its design is that of the wheel, which appears on the abacus of the Sarnath lion capital of Ashoka.
It is the symbol of Justice. 
The National flag was designed by Pingali Venkayya.
The present design of the national flag was adopted by the constituent assembly on 22 July, 1947. 
The description of the importance of three colours and a wheel (chakra) in our national flag by Dr. S. Radhakrishnan is mentioned in the Flag code of India.
The state emblem is an adoption from the Sarnath lion capital of Ashoka.
It was adopted by the Government of India on 26 January, 1950.
The words 'Satyameva Jayate' from Mundaka Upanishad is inscribed at the bottom in the Devnagri script. 
The National Anthem of India 'Jana Gana Mana.....' was officially adopted by the constituent assembly on 24th January, 1950. 
It was first sung on 27th December, 1911 at the Calcutta session of the Indian National Congress (chairman- Pt. Bishan Narayan Dar). 
It was authored by Rabindra Nath Tagore. The Playing time of the full version is approximately 52 seconds. 
The song 'Vande Mataram' was adopted as the national song by the constituent assembly on 24 January, 1950. It is taken from the novel "Anand Math" authored by Bankimchandra Chatterjee. 
It was first sung at the 1896 session of the Indian National Congress (Chairman Rahimtulla Sayani). 
The National Calendar Based on the Saka era (Chaitra as its first month and Falgun as the last) was adopted on 22nd March, 1957 along with the Gregorian Calendar. Generally the month of Chaitra begins on 22 march, but in the leap years, it begins on 21 march.
The National Flower of India is the Lotus (Nelumbo Nucifera Gaertn). The National fruit is Mango (Magnifera Indica).
The National tree is the Banyan tree (Ficus benyhalensis).
While the government has clearly stated in the Rajya Sabha that no notification has been issued regarding India having a National
flower, National fruit and national tree. 
The National Animal is the magnifi cent Tiger (Panthera tigiris). 
The National Bird is the Indian Peacock (Pavo Cristatus). 
The National Aquatic animal is the mammal Ganges River Dolphin (Platanista Gangetica).
The river Ganga was declared the national river by the government of India in the year 2008. 
The Government of India declared the Independence day (15August), Republic day (26 January) and Birth anniversary of Gandhi (2 October) as the National Days.
