- 6th century BC witnessed rise of numerous heterogeneous religions in India, As 62 religious sects were present at that time among which Jainism and Buddhism gained prominence.
- This marked the beginning of second Urbanisation in India, also known as the age of the Buddha.
- In **Post-Vedic period,** <u>Varna system</u> became rigid and it was determined on the basis of birth rather than profession. The society was divided in four Varnas i.e., Brahmin, Kshatriya, Vaishya and Shudra.
- Upliftment in the social hierarchy For this, Vaishyas looked for a new religion which would improve their position.
- The Vaishyas and trader class made generous support to Vardhaman Mahavira and Gautama Buddha.
